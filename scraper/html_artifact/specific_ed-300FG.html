300-300-DW
Research Methods
1 section
  • 00041
  • Laurence Nixon
  • This is the first of the three (3) methodology courses and is normally taken in the student's second term of the program. The goal of the course is to apply the scientific approach to the various social science disciplines. Students identify a research problem and follow the steps to select a research method appropriate to the problem, produce a data collection tool, collect, analyze and interpret the data. The culmination of the process is a research report that presents the research steps in the context of a brief literature review of the topic.

320-216-DW
Natural Environment
1 section
  • 00001
  • Richard Soare
  • This course surveys the major aspects of the physical environment – the biosphere and biodiversity, the atmosphere and climate change and the hydrosphere in relation to uneven distribution of water resources. Through improving ecological knowledge and understanding, future individual and collective decisions can be made less detrimental to the environment. Therefore, while exploring the natural processes that shape the environment, this course will also look at the impacts of human activities and their significance. Potential solutions will be explored.

300-308-DW
Integrative Seminar
1 section
  • The X-Files: The Truth about Belief
  • 00022
  • Richard Soare
  • Questions concerning the origin and possible uniqueness of our place in the universe as sentient and reflective beings date back to the earliest instances of humankind’s introspection. Through the lens of history, philosophy, psychology, religion, etc. this course will explore conventional and unconventional hypotheses about these questions, evaluate their plausibility and attempt to delineate the similarities and differences between “belief,” “truth” and “knowledge”. Part of this process will be the opportunity for students to step into and out of the conceptual “box” in order to dissect commonplace assumptions about who we are, as a species, and whereto we are heading.

360-401-DW
Advanced Environmental Studies
2 sections
  • Sustenance, Sustainability, and the Sacred
  • 00001
  • Natalie Kepka
  • This course will analyse ethical, environmental, and health concerns related to food, nature, and notions of the sacred through the lenses offered by sociology, anthropology, and religious studies. Students will have the opportunity to focus on one modern day environmental food crisis and offer research-based viable solution(s) as to how one would promote and preserve biodiversity, cultural integrity, and sustainability. The emphasis is on providing students with the necessary skills to investigate significant environmental issues using knowledge, concepts, methods and tools necessary to allow them to prepare a pertinent final research based project.

401-225-DW
Sustainability and Business
1 section
  • 00001
  • Helen Stavaris
  • This course introduces students to the array of environmental issues confronting businesses today. Among other things, the student will be introduced to the evolution of environmentalism from a business perspective and the business of environmentalism. The course also examines the cost / profit decisions relating to embracing an eco-friendly business model, the practice of green-washing, as well as legal obligations, legal trends, and ethics.
