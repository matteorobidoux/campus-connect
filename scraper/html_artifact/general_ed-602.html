Important Information

Please refer to the Dawson College website for details on French placement testing requirements.

Pratique du français oral et écrit pour les étudiants non francophones (602-03B-DW or 602-04B-DW): These courses are designed for students from OUTSIDE Quebec only and they are not available during the day (except for students registered in Journeys). If this is your placement, and you need more information or to register, please call the Registration Help Line (514-931-5701).

French block B courses have been designed to meet students' needs in relation to their field of study. These courses will help develop vocabulary and communication skills related to their programs.  Programs have been divided into three "families".

You must take the block B course designed for your program and at your French level.  If you take a block B course this term, find which "family" you belong to, then look at the chart below to find the course number which corresponds to your level and to your "family".

Sciences humaines: Accounting and Management Technology, Business Management (Marketing), Commerce, Community Recreation Leadership Training, Computer Science, Liberal Arts, Social Science, Social Science Profiles, Social Service.

Sciences et techniques:  Biomedical Laboratory Technology, Civil Engineering Technology, Diagnostic Imaging, Electronics Technology, Laboratory Technology/Analytical Chemistry, Mechanical Engineering Technology, Nursing, Physiotherapy Technology, Radiation Oncology, Science.

Dès lors, une question se pose : l’Orientalisme propose-t-il une représentation fidèle de l’Orient? Ou n’en trahit-il pas plutôt une vision occidentale purement fantasmée? Afin de répondre à cette « énigme » et d’alimenter notre réflexion, nous analyserons des tableaux orientalistes (Delacroix, Benjamin-Constant, etc.), nous étudierons des extraits de récits de voyage à Constantinople (Gautier, de Nerval), des poèmes choisis des Orientales de V. Hugo et Les Aventures du dernier Abencérage de F. – R de Chateaubriand. Ce cours se conclura par une création littéraire élaborée en équipe: vous rédigerez vous-même, de manière collaborative, une nouvelle orientaliste illustrée.
