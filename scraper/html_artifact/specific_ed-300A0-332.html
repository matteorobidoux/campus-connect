332-101-DW
Introduction to the Classical World
2 sections
  • Egypt, Greece, and Rome
  • 00001
  • Jiri Tucker
  • This introductory course develops an appreciation of the myths, legends, history, and forces that shaped the world of ancient Egypt, Greece and Rome. Upon completion of this course, students will be able to demonstrate an understanding of methods, such as recognizing ancient texts, language and archaeology, used in Classics. Students should understand the contribution of Classics to specific situations, problems, and questions, including the establishment of democracy, the republic, philosophy, early religion, cult, hierarchies, the development of early literature as well as the contribution and legacy of the Classical World to other historical periods and ideas. Finally, students will develop the basic skills needed to study the Social Sciences, such as completing assignments that develop the ability to take notes and use a textbook.

Evaluation of primary sources in prose, poetry, artifacts, and visual media will be done through an appraisal of these texts. What role does ancient myth really provide?
