  • Sustenance, Sustainability, and the Sacred
  • 00001
  • Natalie Kepka
  • This course will analyse ethical, environmental, and health concerns related to food, nature, and notions of the sacred through the lenses offered by sociology, anthropology, and religious studies. Students will have the opportunity to focus on one modern day environmental food crisis and offer research-based viable solution(s) as to how one would promote and preserve biodiversity, cultural integrity, and sustainability. The emphasis is on providing students with the necessary skills to investigate significant environmental issues using knowledge, concepts, methods and tools necessary to allow them to prepare a pertinent final research based project.
